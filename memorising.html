Memorising Tips

Everyone is different therefore their memory and strategies will be different, here are some of the most common memorisation strategies:

  • Mindmaps
  • Flashcards
  • Blurting
  • Teach it
  • Traffic light system
Mindmaps: help to visually see the content you need to memorise, this helps a lot to condense information for end-of-year exams. You can create branches for each area of study then their subtopics and then the key knowledge.

Flashcards: digitally or physically writing a question/term on one side and the answer on the other. This helps quiz yourself and memorise specific condensed information e.g. terminology.

Blurting: is when you give yourself a topic/subject and you write down as much information you remember about it (with no time limit). Then once you are done look over that topic and find the information you missed and study those until you remember everything.

Teach: when you teach someone else about the topic/subject, this helps with memorisation and finding the gaps in your knowledge

Traffic light system: when you go through your notes with a red, orange, and green highlighter.
